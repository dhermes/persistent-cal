This is what I like to call a "missing feature" of Google Calendar. I was inspired
by the fact that my coworkers could not see my flights with the rest of
my work calendar. Rather than sharing my TripIt link with all my coworkers (even
those who I didn't know needed my travel plans), why not just import my TripIt into
my work calendar? But by using the "Import Calendar" option in Google
Calendar, it is only a static upload and updates won't persist. Enter persistant-cal!

Silly change untweak.

The following tries to cover some possible questions.

  • You may only request up to four calendar feeds.
  • Only calendar feeds from a predefined whitelist will be used. All other feeds you supply will be ignored.
  • At this time, the whitelist is the following list of regular expressions:
    • ^((http|https|webcal)://|)www.tripit.com/feed/ical/private/[A-Za-z0-9-]+/tripit.ics$
  • Each event in your calendar(s) will be added to a private general purpose Google Calendar.
  • You will be added as an attendee to the events specific to your feeds, but will not have edit privileges over the Google Calendar events.
  • If another user has a feed with the exact same ID, they will also be added to the Google Calendar event.
